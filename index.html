Since 1974

Keepers of the Analog Groove

The Wax Journal is more than a record store. It is a sanctuary for crate diggers, a community hub, and a living museum.

Tucked into the historic corner of the downtown district, our dusty oak shelves carry decades of cultural history. Here, every record has a story before you even drop the needle.

"Music shouldn't just be streamed. It deserves to be held, cared for, and experienced in full fidelity."

— Arthur Pendleton, Founder

Our Mission

To preserve the tactile and communal experience of analog music. We aim to protect independent music culture, provide a welcoming space for discovery, and ensure classic sounds continue to inspire listeners.

A Journey in Sound

  1. 1974

    The Basement Crates

    Arthur Pendleton opens a small underground cellar shop selling hand-cleaned soul and jazz records.

  2. 1995

    Defying the CD Era

    As the industry shifts entirely to digital CDs, we double down on our wax inventory, keeping vinyl alive.

  3. Present

    The Analog Revival

    We continue welcoming new generations of audiophiles with modern pressings and active listening nights.
